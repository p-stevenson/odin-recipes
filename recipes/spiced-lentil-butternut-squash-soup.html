Spiced lentil & butternut squash soup

Description

A deliciously heartwarming soup! Cook a big batch and freeze the leftovers for a speedy supper another day. It's healthy and low in fat.

  • 2 tbsp olive oil
  • 2 onions, finely chopped
  • 2 garlic cloves, crushed
  • 1 tbsp ras el hanout
  • 1 butternut squash, peeled and cut into 2cm pieces
  • 100g red lentils
  • 1l hot vegetable stock
  • 1 small bunch coriander leaves, chopped, plus extra to serve
  • 1 tbsp hot chilli powder
  • dukkah (see tip) and natural yogurt, to serve

Steps

    • Heat the oil in a large flameproof casserole dish or sausepan over a medium-high heat.
    • Fry the onions with a pinch of salt for 7 mins, or until softened and just caramelised.
    • Add the garlic, chilli and ras el hanout and cook for 1 min more.
    • Stir in the squash and lentils.
    • Pour over the stock and season to taste.
    • Bring to the boil, then reduce the heat to a simmer and cook, covered, for 25 mins or until the squash is soft.
    • Blitz the soup with a stick blender until smooth, then season to taste.
    • To freeze, leave to cool completely and transfer to large freezerproof bags
    • Stir in the coriander leaves and ladle the soup into bowls
    • Serve topped with the dukkah, yogurt and extra coriander leaves.
